# Soft Monogram Design Philosophy

Soft Monogram treats the favicon as a compressed identity mark rather than a miniature interface. The canvas should feel calm, deliberate, and instantly legible at browser-tab scale. One form carries the full burden of recognition, so that form must be confident, quiet, and unmistakably shaped.

Space is not decorative background here; it is part of the letter. The negative space around the monogram must create breathing room and preserve clarity when the icon is reduced to sixteen pixels. The mark should feel centered without becoming rigid, and rounded without becoming childish.

Color should preserve the site's editorial mood. A deep mint field acts as the stable surface, while a warm off-white letter supplies contrast and softness. The palette should feel paper-like and composed, not glossy, bright, or app-like. Visual restraint is more valuable than novelty.

Scale and rhythm come from weight distribution inside the letter itself. The vertical stem should feel grounded, while the hook of the `J` should soften the silhouette and make the monogram memorable. The curve should suggest handwriting discipline without imitating script.

Composition must prioritize icon recognition over typographic expression. The final result should look refined in a tab bar, bookmarks list, and mobile shortcut. It should read as an elegant signature for a personal notebook: meticulous, warm, and quietly distinctive.
